Foundational Design

From Paper to Digital, a chance to try a myriad of tools to build many prototypes.


After that, I started work on some reasonably high fidelity prototypes with UXPin, during which I started to play with the colors that I might use for the final product, and what sort of interactions I wanted to work with.


To me, it looks kind of tacky, but the essentials still hold very well to what it was meant to accomplish. I can see a few features that persisted to the current version, but since this version there have been enough structural changes that this has become a nostalic memory.


It's important to realize that each project starts rough and becomes refined over time. This was a good first step, but UXPin proved to be too weak to perform the future tasks at this stage of the design.


The prototype in working condition, I moved on to a usability test.
